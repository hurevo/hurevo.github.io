# How We Work
Hurevo uses a clear, repeatable workflow that matches modern software development and agency best practices.

**1. Discover & Qualify**
Hurevo starts with a short conversation and a simple requirements form to understand the business goals, current tools, budget range, and urgency. Based on clarity and complexity, Hurevo recommends the right engagement model—fixed‑price project, time‑and‑materials, prebuilt solution pack, or continuous development retainer.

**2. Proposal & Agreement**
Hurevo prepares a concise proposal outlining goals, scope, suggested solution, timeline, and pricing. Once aligned, this is formalized with a Master Service Agreement and a Statement of Work that clearly define deliverables, milestones, and acceptance criteria to avoid scope creep and confusion later.

**3. Discovery & Design**
For non‑trivial projects, Hurevo runs a focused discovery phase: workshops, process mapping, user flows, and technical review. The outcome is a refined requirements/backlog and a simple architecture or process design, reflecting industry guidance that a proper discovery phase greatly reduces delivery risk.

**4. Build, Integrate & Iterate**
Hurevo works in short iterations (sprints), using AI‑assisted and low‑code tools where possible to accelerate development and integration. At the end of each iteration, clients see a demo, provide feedback, and help prioritize the next steps, following agile and iterative SDLC best practices instead of a rigid one‑shot delivery.

**5. Launch, Stabilize & Adjust**
After internal testing and client UAT, Hurevo deploys the solution and supports a stabilization period. During this time, clients receive a clearly scoped free adjustment session (for small changes) plus the option to roll into a support or continuous development plan.

**6. Continuous Development & Support**
For clients who want ongoing evolution rather than one‑off delivery, Hurevo offers retainers covering maintenance, small improvements, new features, and further automations. Retainer‑style continuous work is widely used by successful agencies and IT service firms because it provides stability for both sides and supports long‑term value creation.
